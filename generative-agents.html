Simulacra of believable human behaviors

Generative Agents provide an LLM-based agent architecture designed to simulate human behaviors within a sandbox environment. Because human behavior is vast, dynamic, and context-dependent, traditional agents often struggle to appear genuinely believable. Generative Agents memorize past experiences, reflect on them, and generate plans and actions that remain coherent in the moment and over time — they can wake up, cook, head to work, coordinate parties, and improvise conversations with other agents.

Role-play & memory

Role-play prompts

Agent descriptions are composed of three aspects: basic information (name, age, innate traits), current state, and lifestyle. These aspects can be adapted per task. For example, Isabella Rodriguez is a 34-year-old café owner preparing a Valentine's Day party, and her persona guides all downstream behaviors.

Structured memory

Memory lives in a JSON database with three types: event, chat, and thought. Retrieval ranks memories by recency, relevance (via cosine similarity), and importance (LLM-rated). This keeps the most meaningful experiences salient without overloading the context window.

Example · Rating memory importance

Task: "On a scale of 1 to 10, rate the poignancy of: buying groceries at The Willows Market and Pharmacy."

Output: 3

Planning

Plans specify a future sequence of actions that promote consistent behavior. Daily plans are generated based on each agent's character profile and current state, then executed sequentially with dynamic adjustments as the day unfolds.

Example · Daily plan prompt

Today is (current date). Here is Isabella's plan today in broad strokes:
06:00 AM: Wake up and complete morning routine
07:00 AM: Prepare breakfast and review final details for the party
08:00 AM: Open Hobbs Cafe and greet early customers
...
11:00 PM: Clean up with staff and reflect on the success of the event

Reflection

Over time, memory may accumulate redundant records. Generative Agents therefore reflect on extended chats and summarize them into higher-level thoughts that help future interactions. After Isabella speaks with Francisco about the show, the LLM condenses their excitement into a single sentence that keeps the party preparations on track.

Example · Condensing a conversation

"Isabella Rodriguez and Francisco Lopez are discussing their excitement for the Valentine's Day party at Hobbs Cafe and working together to make his performance engaging and memorable."
