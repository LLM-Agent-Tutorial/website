Agent Safety

Deployed agents must stay aligned, controllable, and recoverable. The paper’s checklist covers permissioning, hardened infrastructure, resilient rollbacks, and supervisory agents that monitor for rogue behavior.

Protection checklist

Combine organizational controls with technical safeguards so agents can be trusted in production environments.

  • Permission management: Restrict the tools, credentials, and APIs that an autonomous agent can invoke. Principle of least privilege limits the blast radius of mistakes.
  • Secure components: Harden every boundary—sandbox extensions, audited MCP channels, and encrypted storage—so attackers cannot escalate through the agent stack.
  • Resilience and recovery: Snapshot critical state, plan for rollbacks, and define fallback policies that safely pause or restart agents when anomalies occur.
  • Supervisory agents: Dedicated overseers in a multi-agent system can detect collusion, enforce policy, and override actions when downstream agents drift from their charter.
