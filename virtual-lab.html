AI–human collaboration in medical research

The Virtual Lab facilitates interdisciplinary research through collaboration between AI agents and human researchers. The human defines two primary agents — a Principal Investigator (PI) and a Scientific Critic. The PI assembles a specialized team and orchestrates both team and individual meetings to design a comprehensive workflow for the research topic. Humans then execute the final scripts to complete the project.

Role-play & reflection

The PI is charged with maximizing scientific impact, making key decisions, and managing timelines. The Scientific Critic offers targeted feedback after each response, refining team assignments and preventing blind spots.

Example · Team generation excerpt

Agent 1 — Computational Biologist. Goal: Develop and refine predictive models for protein-antibody interactions.

The Scientific Critic immediately responds with targeted suggestions before execution begins.

Cooperation

Team meetings follow a structured agenda supplied by the human. The PI opens, specialist agents respond in rounds, and the PI summarizes, recommends decisions, and lists next steps. The human can understand outcomes by reading only the PI's summary.

Example · Agenda capture

Agenda question: Will you design standard antibodies or nanobodies?
Summary answer: "Nanobodies. They offer superior stability, tissue penetration, and..."

Tool use

After the meeting, the team collaboratively chooses computational tools (AlphaFold, Rosetta, etc.). The PI then assigns tools during individual meetings, and specialist agents generate structured scripts with human-in-the-loop guidance. The process yields complete RosettaScripts XML, command lines, and helper Python to post-process scores, all ready for researchers to execute.

Example · Tool brainstorming snippet

"Apply AlphaFold or Rosetta to model the 3D structures of the top-ranked nanobody variants..."

The snippet above is recorded during the tool generation meeting and later translated into executable scripts.
