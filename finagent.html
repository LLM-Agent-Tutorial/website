FinAgent: A Multimodal Foundation Agent for Financial Trading

Paper ↗ GitHub ↗

FinAgent is a multimodal foundation agent designed for financial trading that addresses adaptability and generalization through a market intelligence module, a reflection module, and a tool-augmented decision-making module. It extracts and summarizes insights from diverse market information, performs dual-level reflection on market dynamics and past decisions, and enhances trading actions through expert-informed guidance.

Role-play

FinAgent is guided by a high-level role description that ensures consistent expertise in market analysis and financial decision-making. The role prompt defines the agent's role, the context of the financial market environment, and the agent's analytical capabilities for trading.

Example of role-playing as an expert financial trader

Role: You are an expert trader who has sufficient financial experience and provides expert guidance.

Context: Imagine working in a real market environment with access to real-time prices, news, financial reports, technical indicators, and auxiliary strategies.

Capabilities: You are capable of deeply analyzing, understanding, and summarizing information to make informed trading decisions (BUY, HOLD, SELL).

Memory

Memory plays a crucial role in FinAgent. It adopts a vector-based storage architecture consisting of three main components: (i) summary of market intelligence, collecting and analyzing key insights from multimodal data; (ii) summary of low-level reflection, identifying potential patterns in price movements; and (iii) summary of high-level reflection, storing lessons from past trading decisions. These summaries are fed into subsequent prompts to maintain context over time.

Reflection

FinAgent incorporates a dual-level reflection mechanism. Low-level reflection analyzes price change data to reason about and identify potential patterns in market movements. High-level reflection focuses on past trading decisions, summarizing overarching lessons to recommend improvements and corrective actions.

Example of analyzing market dynamics with low-level reflection

INPUT: Summaries of latest market intelligence, summaries of past intelligence, Kline chart, and task instructions to analyze reasoning that led to price movements.

OUTPUT: reasoning: "The short-term price increase of 1.60% for Apple Inc. (AAPL) can be attributed to recent positive market sentiment surrounding Apple's upcoming AR/VR product line..." query: "Short-term growth in AAPL's price is driven by reactions to product excitement and concerns..."

Example of analyzing past decisions with high-level reflection

INPUT: Summaries of latest and past market intelligence, reasoning from low-level reflection, historical actions, trading chart, and task instructions.

OUTPUT: reasoning: "The BUY decision made on 2023-06-01 appears to have been correct..." improvement: "Constant vigilance and responsiveness ... setting stop-losses to mitigate risks." summary: "The success of the BUY decision ... underscores the importance of comprehensive market analysis..." query: "The price movement analysis and technical indicators provided a compelling case for a bullish trend..."
