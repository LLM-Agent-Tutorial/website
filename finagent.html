A multimodal foundation agent for financial trading

FinAgent addresses adaptability and generalization through three components: a market intelligence module that summarizes diverse nancial information, a reection module that reasons about market dynamics and past decisions, and a tool-augmented decision-making module with expert guidance for actions.

Role-play & memory

Role prompt: "You are an expert trader... Imagine working in a real market environment where you have access to daily prices, news, reports, sentiment, technical indicators, and auxiliary strategies." The agent must make informed BUY/HOLD/SELL calls.

Memory adopts a vector-based storage architecture with three components: summaries of market intelligence, summaries of low-level reection (patterns in price movements), and summaries of high-level reection (lessons from prior trades). These summaries feed later prompts.

Reflection

Low-level reection analyzes price change data to reason about short-, medium-, and long-term drivers. High-level reection inspects past trading decisions, labeling them right or wrong and suggesting improvements.

Example · Low-level reflection

"The short-term price increase of 1.60% for Apple Inc. can be attributed to recent positive market sentiment surrounding Apple's upcoming AR/VR product line..."

query: Short-term growth in AAPL's price is driven by reactions to product excitement...

Example · Assessing a BUY call

Improvement plan: "Future scenarios should involve dynamic trade adjustments such as setting stop-losses."
